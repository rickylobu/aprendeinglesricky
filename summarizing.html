Summarizing - Resumiendo

Summarizing is the art of capturing the main points and essential details of a text in a concise and condensed form.

Resumir es el arte de capturar los puntos principales y los detalles esenciales de un texto de forma concisa y condensada.

  1. Identify the Main Idea:
    • Before summarizing, understand the central theme or main idea of the text. What is the author trying to convey?
  2. Highlight Key Points:
    • Identify the crucial points, arguments, or events that support the main idea. These are the building blocks of your summary.
  3. Omit Unnecessary Details:
    • Focus on the essential information and omit irrelevant or redundant details. Keep it streamlined and to the point.
  4. Paraphrase:
    • Express the main points in your own words. This demonstrates your understanding of the material and helps create a concise summary.
  5. Maintain Logical Order:
    • Present the information in a logical sequence. Your summary should flow smoothly, capturing the original text's structure.
  6. Check for Clarity:
    • Ensure that your summary is clear and coherent. Avoid jargon or overly complex language. Aim for simplicity without losing the original meaning.
  1. Identificar la Idea Principal:
    • Antes de resumir, comprende el tema central o la idea principal del texto. ¿Qué intenta transmitir el autor?
  2. Destacar Puntos Clave:
    • Identifica los puntos cruciales, argumentos o eventos que respaldan la idea principal. Estos son los elementos fundamentales de tu resumen.
  3. Omitir Detalles Innecesarios:
    • Concéntrate en la información esencial y omite detalles irrelevantes o redundantes. Mantén el resumen simplificado y al grano.
  4. Parafrasear:
    • Expresa los puntos principales con tus propias palabras. Esto demuestra tu comprensión del material y ayuda a crear un resumen conciso.
  5. Mantener un Orden Lógico:
    • Presenta la información en una secuencia lógica. Tu resumen debe fluir de manera coherente, capturando la estructura del texto original.
  6. Verificar la Claridad:
    • Asegúrate de que tu resumen sea claro y coherente. Evita jerga o lenguaje demasiado complejo. Apunta a la simplicidad sin perder el significado original.

AI Definitions


The key words in the definition of AI by practitioners are machines (19%), learning (17%), perform tasks (15%), human intelligence (13%) and computer systems (12%). This is because, generally, practitioners perceive AI to be machines or computer systems capable of learning to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence. However, there are some particularities, depending on the expert group.
Most IT companies perceive AI as a term in the computer science field used to describe machines that perform tasks by mimicking human intelligence using abilities acquired through machine learning and deep learning. Consulting firms define AI more like the theory and development of computer systems (mostly machines) or technologies capable of performing tasks that normally require human intelligence.
Industry analysts often refer to AI as a set of technologies, machines or systems capable of emulating human performance typically by learning to understand complex data that normally requires cognition. Reviews and magazines often present AI as the science and engineering of self-aware intelligent machines that think like humans by solving complex problems and performing tasks usually associated with intelligent humans.

AI Dimensions and Application Domains


Practitioners tend to classify AI into two main dimensions which some also called types, categories, forms or domains: artificial general Intelligence (AGI – 33%), and artificial narrow Intelligence (ANI - 26%). AGI, which some referred to as general/generalized AI, Pure AI or Strong AI, is AI that can perform any/multiple intellectual tasks in a way that matches or surpasses human intelligence. ANI, which some practitioners referred to as narrow/applied AI, Pragmatic AI, or Weak AI refers to the application of AI techniques to narrower or specific problems.
Practitioners claim they started to focus on ANI because of the lack of progress they were making in AGI. PwC suggests Hardwired/specific systems and Adaptive systems as the two main dimensions of AI. Hardwired systems have automated and assisted intelligence capabilities, while adaptive systems have augmented and autonomous intelligence capabilities.
According to IBM, AI has two dimensions: the one where AI emulates the human thought process or intelligent behavior, and the other where it achieves the ideal human performance. Oracle uses the term artificial superintelligence (ASI) to describe AI with intelligence that exceeds human capacity, limiting AGI to AI with general human intelligence.
Deloitte emphasized on three application domains: Cognitive Automation (deep domain-specific expertise), Cognitive Engagement (using cognitive technology to engage with people), and Cognitive Insight (using data to generate personalized and relevant information). Capgemini found that most AI technologies implemented today fall under three categories: Natural Language Processing (NLP e.g. Chatbots / Voicebots), Biometrics (e.g. image/video analysis, voice recognition), and Technology Foundation (e.g. Machine Learning).

AI Characteristics


We identified two main functions or characteristics of AI: learning (26%) and perception (26%). In fact, there is no AI if the system cannot learn and perceive data or objects in its environment. AI must be able to learn from experience, and use the learning to comprehend (16%) by recognizing patterns, solve complex problems, understand language and its nuances, create perspectives and make decisions. Also, it must demonstrate some degree of environmental perception such as visual perception and speech recognition.
Some consulting firms mentioned enhanced data processing (EDP – 10%), natural language processing (NLP – 11%) and problem-solving (11%) as core AI characteristics. However, reading further shows that EDP and NLP are actually ways AI gets to learn and perceive its environment using Big data. Given that systems are generally built as solutions to problems, stating problem-solving as a characteristic of AI requires more investigation.

AI Capabilities


As an emerging technology, AI is expected to have promising capabilities that will enable or facilitate the transformation or creation of businesses processes and industrial systems. However, these capabilities have not been widely discussed (Brynjolfsson et al. 2018). The four main AI capabilities (or cognitive services) discussed by practitioners are: learn (25%), sense (18%), act (18%), and comprehend (18%). These capabilities came mostly from consulting firms, especially Accenture, PwC, KPMG, Capgemini and Deloitte. Sense as AI’s ability to process information like images, sound, speech, and text; comprehend as AI’s pattern recognition capabilities; act as AI’s ability to interpret data, make rational decisions and execute them automatically; and learn as AI’s ability to leverage algorithms for the interpretation of input data. Microsoft and IBM perceive vision (5%), speech (5%), language (6%), knowledge (5%) and search to be the main AI
capabilities. They call them the main building blocks of every AI-based solution. Vision refers to AI’s ability to recognize image or video content; speech refers to its ability to understand sounds and transcribe them into text; language refers to their ability to understand the meaning of words despite the nuances and complexities of language; and knowledge refers to its ability to understand relationships between objects in its environment.
Perceptive capabilities like vision and speech, and cognitive capabilities like learning and knowledge (reasoning) are at the center of human performance. These capabilities could be an opportunity for IS researchers to propose new applications of AI-enabled ICTs for people and organizations. For example, patient care could really benefit from the excellent sensing capabilities of robotic systems to perform interactive tasks. Practitioners do not lay sufficient emphasis on the knowledge sharing capabilities of AI. This is particularly important for advancing the operational and managerial impact of AI on information systems by increasing the self-improvement rates of these systems. Thus, IS researchers could also carry out studies that focus on the knowledge-sharing capabilities of AI and how they can be used to solve real-world problems.

AI Principles


We observed that there is no generally accepted basis for good AI reasoning, conduct or behavior. Many practitioners did not focus on this aspect and those who did suggest principles in line with their perspective and applications of AI. However, bias/fairness (16%), transparency (14%), privacy (14%), accountability (12%), safety/reliability (10%), and security (5%) were the most recurrent principles expected to guide the design and development of AI. The principles suggested and applied by IT companies mostly have an orientation towards AI design and development. More or less in addition to the aforementioned principles, IBM, for example, suggests other principles like value alignment, explainable, and user data rights. They also talk about intent, algorithmic and data responsibility, system assurance, embedded values, robustness. SAP adds principles like value driven, design for people, and societal engagement. Google proposes principles like socially beneficial, and high scientific standards. Consulting firms like Accenture, EY, and Cognizant suggested more business-oriented AI principles. These include transform relationships, re-imagine business models and processes, illuminate dark data, trust, control, purposeful, agile and vigilant, performance, and personalization.

AI Technologies


To refer to AI technologies, practitioners interchangeably used terms like subfields, application areas, methods, techniques, and branches. The most widely known AI technologies among practitioners include machine learning (ML – 27%), deep learning (DL – 16%), NLP (14%), computer vision (8%), robotics (5%), speech recognition (5%), and neural networks (4%). IT companies were mostly interested in ML and DL, with a few discussing neural networks, NLP and computer vision. All other AI technologies like text analysis, speech recognition, and smart robotics were not very much discussed. The main technologies discussed by consulting firms were NLP, ML, computer vision, and DL. Experts reviews and magazines focused on DL and ML, while research institutions focused on DL and NLP.
(AWS 2018) suggests that there are five main approaches to machine learning: symbolists, connectionists, evolutionaries, Bayesians, and analogizers. These approaches can be categorized into supervised and unsupervised learning techniques. This information provides guidelines for IS researchers interested in conducting cutting-edge research on AI technologies. ML, DL, and NLP are the top three areas of interest for all expert groups. Computer vision, robotics, speech recognition, and neural networks are also of interest to industry experts but not as much. There are several other AI technologies but they are not the primary focus of the companies in this study.
